Things to Know Before You Buy

  • Size matters more than features. A mirror that shows your whole face and shoulders lets you blend makeup evenly without leaning in. Aim for at least 24 inches wide if you have the wall space.
  • None of these have built-in lights. The best bathroom mirrors for makeup pair a flat, distortion-free pane with strong, even light from your fixtures or a window, so plan your lighting alongside the mirror.
  • Frameless or framed is a style call. Frameless mirrors suit any decor and cost less, while a framed model like the Moen matches metal hardware and hides the mounting clips.
  • Flat glass beats magnified glass for daily use. A full flat mirror shows your face in true proportion, so save a small magnifying mirror for fine detail like brows and lashes.
  • Measure your wall first. The widths here run from 15 to 36 inches, so check your vanity and stud spacing before you buy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size bathroom mirror is best for makeup?

For makeup, a mirror at least 24 inches wide lets you see your whole face and shoulders so you can blend evenly without leaning in. Our top pick, the Ruomeng frameless mirror, measures 32 by 24 inches, which suits most single vanities. If wall space is tight, the 16-by-24-inch KOCUUY still shows your face clearly.

Do these bathroom mirrors come with lights?

No. Every mirror in this guide is a plain wall mirror without built-in lighting. For makeup you want strong, even light, so mount your mirror across from a window or under a vanity light bar. Avoid relying on a single overhead light, which casts shadows under your eyes and chin.

Is a frameless or framed mirror better for a makeup vanity?

Both work, so it comes down to style and budget. Frameless mirrors like the Ruomeng and KOCUUY suit any decor and cost less. A framed model like the Moen Glenshire matches brushed-nickel hardware and hides the mounting clips for a finished look. The reflection quality is the same as long as the glass is flat and tempered.

Can you use a regular wall mirror for applying makeup?

Yes. A flat, distortion-free wall mirror shows your face in true color and proportion, which is what matters most for everyday makeup. A small magnifying mirror helps for fine detail like brows and lashes, but you do not need one to put on makeup well.
