Things to Know Before You Buy

  • Magnification matters more than size. A 5x to 8x mirror shows the stray hairs a flat wall mirror hides, which is why three of our four picks magnify.
  • Mounting style changes your setup. An extendable arm lets you pull the mirror to your face, while a fixed wall panel keeps you at a set distance from the glass.
  • Glass quality controls distortion. Tempered glass with a true silvered backing keeps the reflection flat so your jawline does not warp at the edges.
  • Lighting comes from your bathroom. None of these picks light up, so place the mirror where it faces your vanity bulbs rather than away from them.
  • Price tracks size and reach, not always clarity. Our cheapest pick costs $6.79 and our priciest $59.99, and both reflect cleanly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What magnification is best for a shaving mirror?

For shaving, 5x to 8x hits the sweet spot. It shows individual hairs along the neck and jaw without distorting the rest of your face the way 10x and higher mirrors do. Our top pick, the DECLUTTR, sits in that range.

How do I keep a bathroom mirror for shaving from fogging?

Run the bathroom fan during your shower, shave before the room heats up, or wipe the glass with a thin smear of shaving cream and buff it clear. Small magnifying mirrors fog faster than large wall panels, so give them a few seconds to clear first.

Do I need a magnifying mirror if I already have a wall mirror?

If you shave close and want to catch stray hairs, yes. A flat wall mirror keeps you at arm's length, while a magnifying mirror like our picks pulls the detail in. Many people pair the two, using the wall mirror for the full view and a magnifying or hand mirror for the close work.
