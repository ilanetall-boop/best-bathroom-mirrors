Things to Know Before You Buy

  • A double vanity usually needs more than one mirror. You hang a main mirror over each sink, or one wide mirror across both, then add a magnifying mirror for close work. Budget for the pair plus an accessory mirror, not a single purchase.
  • Match the main mirror width to your sink, not your wall. A common rule keeps each mirror a few inches narrower than the vanity section below it. The LOAAO here is 22 inches wide, which suits a standard 24-to-30-inch sink cabinet.
  • Magnifying mirrors solve what wall mirrors cannot. A mirror over the sink sits 8 to 12 inches from your face, too far for eyebrow shaping, contact lenses, or close shaving. A small magnifying mirror fills that gap.
  • Tempered glass is the spec that matters in a bathroom. Every mirror in this guide uses tempered glass, which handles the heat and humidity swings near a shower and breaks into blunt pieces instead of shards if it ever fails.
  • Decide between framed and frameless before you shop. A black-framed rectangle like the LOAAO reads modern and hides edge wear; a frameless makeup or hand mirror keeps the focus on the reflection. Both suit a double vanity if you keep finishes consistent.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should a double vanity have one mirror or two?

Either works, and it comes down to your wall and your style. Two matching mirrors, one over each sink, give a symmetrical built-in look and ship and hang more easily than a single oversized sheet. One wide mirror spanning both sinks reads more open and modern. For most double vanities we recommend a matching pair like the LOAAO 22x30, since you can replace one without redoing the whole wall.

What size mirror fits a double vanity?

Keep each mirror a few inches narrower than the sink section below it. For a standard 24-to-30-inch sink cabinet, a 22-inch-wide mirror like the LOAAO sits in the right proportion. If you hang one continuous mirror across a double vanity, match it to the full vanity width and leave a small margin on each side.

Do I need a magnifying mirror on a double vanity?

A magnifying mirror is not required, but it solves what the main mirrors cannot. A mirror over the sink sits 8 to 12 inches from your face, too far for eyebrow shaping, contact lenses, or a close shave. Adding one wall-mounted magnifying mirror, like the DECLUTTR or BTremary, covers that detail work without cluttering the counter.
