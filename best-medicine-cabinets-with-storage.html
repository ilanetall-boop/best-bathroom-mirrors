Things to Know Before You Buy

  • Surface-mount vs recessed: Surface-mount cabinets give you more storage and skip the wall cutting, but they stick out a few inches. Recessed models sit flush and need a stud cavity.
  • Mirror size should match your vanity: A 16 to 24-inch cabinet suits a single sink; a 40 to 50-inch model spans a double vanity.
  • LED and anti-fog add cost, not storage: Lighting and defoggers push the price toward $100 to $200 but do not change how much you can fit inside.
  • Check the door swing and hinge side: Most of these cabinets ship with a single reversible or fixed door, so confirm it opens away from a wall or toward your dominant hand.
  • Tempered glass is the norm: Every pick here uses a tempered-glass mirror, which resists chips and is safer if it ever breaks.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are recessed or surface-mount medicine cabinets better for storage?

Surface-mount cabinets give you more usable storage because the full box sits proud of the wall, so shelves run the cabinet's whole depth. Recessed cabinets sit flush and look tidier, but you lose depth to the wall cavity and you have to cut into studs. If storage is your priority and you can accept the cabinet sticking out a few inches, go surface-mount. Five of our seven picks, including the Venlus, install surface-mount with no wall cutting.

How much does a medicine cabinet with storage cost?

A solid medicine cabinet with storage runs from about $60 to $200. Our budget pick, the CHOEZON medicine mirror cabinet, costs $59.99 with a plain tempered-glass mirror. Adding LED lighting and anti-fog moves you to the $75 to $100 range, where the Briivue and Ratsamee sit. Oversized lit cabinets like the LOAAO reach $199. You pay mainly for lighting, mirror size, and defogger features, not for shelf storage itself.

What size medicine cabinet do I need?

Match the cabinet width to your vanity. For a single sink, a 16 to 24-inch-wide cabinet like the Venlus or Briivue covers the mirror you need without crowding the wall. For a double vanity, step up to a 40 to 50-inch model such as the Delma or LOAAO so the mirror spans both sinks. Measure your wall space first, leave a few inches of clearance on each side, and check that a surface-mount box will not block a light fixture or window trim.

Can you install a medicine cabinet with storage yourself?

Yes, in most cases. The plain surface-mount cabinets like the Venlus and both CHOEZON models hang on wall anchors, so a confident DIYer can mount one in under an hour with a drill and a level. The LED models add wiring, which means connecting the cabinet to power and following the lighting instructions, so budget more time and call an electrician if you are not comfortable working near a circuit. For the large lit units, line up a helper to hold the weight while you fix it level.
