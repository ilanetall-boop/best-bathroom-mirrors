Things to Know Before You Buy

  • Magnification matters more than size for makeup. A 5X mirror lets you see brow hairs and liner edges that a flat wall mirror blurs at arm's length. The best vanity mirrors for makeup pair a magnified view with a normal 1X view.
  • Lighting changes your color. Foundation that looks right under a yellow bathroom bulb can read orange in daylight. A lighted mirror with adjustable color temperature keeps your makeup consistent from room to room.
  • Decide tabletop or wall first. Tabletop mirrors sit on a vanity and often magnify, while wall mirrors give a full head-and-shoulders view but no close-up help. Many people keep one of each.
  • Check the surface size, not just the footprint. A 13-by-5-inch tabletop shows your face, while a 36-by-24-inch wall mirror shows your whole upper body.
  • Prices here run from $25.99 to $109.99. Lighted mirrors and matched sets cost more, while a simple tiltable tabletop stays under $30.

We looked at several other types of vanity mirrors for makeup before settling on these seven, and a few common categories did not make the cut.

Hollywood-style bulb mirrors add a row of round globe lights for a vanity-table look, but the ones we checked ran hot, drew more power, and cost well above our lighted picks for no better color accuracy. The ROLOVE gives you even light for less.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best vanity mirror for makeup?

For most people, the DOWRY 5X Tabletop is the best vanity mirror for makeup. Its double-sided design gives you 5X magnification for detail work and a 1X view for your whole face, and the weighted base keeps it steady. If your room is dark, add a lighted mirror like the ROLOVE for even color.

Do I need a lighted vanity mirror for makeup?

You need good light, and a lighted mirror is the easiest way to get it if your bathroom bulbs run yellow or dim. A lighted model with adjustable color temperature, such as the ROLOVE or the budget NUSVAN, keeps your foundation looking the same indoors as it will in daylight. If you already do your makeup near a window, an unlit tabletop mirror works fine.

What magnification is best for applying makeup?

5X magnification, like the DOWRY's, handles most makeup tasks: brows, liner, and blending concealer. Pair it with a 1X view so you can step back and check your whole face. Higher magnifications like 10X help with very fine work but show too small an area for everyday use.

Should a makeup mirror be wall-mounted or sit on the counter?

It depends on where you get ready. A tabletop mirror that magnifies suits a seated vanity and close detail work, while a large wall mirror like the Keonjinn or LOAAO suits anyone who does makeup standing at the sink. Many people keep a magnifying tabletop next to a larger wall mirror so they get both views.
